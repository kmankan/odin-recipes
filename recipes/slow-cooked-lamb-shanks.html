~ Slow Cooked Lamb Shanks in Red Wine Sauce ~

Description

Indulge in the ultimate comfort food with these melt-in-your-mouth Slow Cooked Lamb Shanks in a rich and flavorful Red Wine Sauce. This dish is perfect for a cozy dinner with family and friends, or for a special occasion where you want to impress your guests. The lamb shanks are slowly braised in a medley of aromatic vegetables, robust red wine, and fragrant herbs, resulting in tender, fall-off-the-bone meat that's bursting with flavor. The luscious sauce, infused with the essence of red wine and tomatoes, is so divine that you'll want to mop up every last drop with a piece of crusty bread. Served over a bed of creamy mashed potatoes or polenta, this dish is a true showstopper that will have everyone asking for seconds. Don't be intimidated by the steps - the slow cooking process is straightforward and foolproof, guaranteeing a delicious and satisfying meal every time.

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb shanks, around 1 lb/450g each
  • 2 tsp salt, divided
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 2 - 3 tbsp olive oil, separated
  • 1 cup onion, finely diced
  • 1 cup carrot, finely diced
  • 1 cup celery, finely diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 1/2 cups red wine
  • 800 g canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 5 sprigs of thyme, or 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 dried bay leaves or 4 fresh

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
  2. Pat the lamb shanks dry and sprinkle with 1 tsp of salt and black pepper.
  3. Heat 2 tbsp of olive oil in a heavy based pot over high heat. Sear the lamb shanks in 2 batches until brown all over, about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove lamb onto a plate and drain excess fat (if any) from the pot.
  5. Turn the heat down to medium-low. Heat remaining 1 tbsp of olive oil in the same pot. Add the onion, carrot, celery, and garlic. Sauté for 10 minutes until the vegetables are softened.
  6. Add the red wine, turn up the heat to medium-high, and bring it to a simmer. Let it simmer for 2 minutes.
  7. Add the remaining ingredients (including remaining 1 tsp of salt) and stir to combine.
  8. Place the lamb shanks into the pot, squeezing them in to fit so they are mostly submerged.
  9. Turn the stove up, bring back up to a simmer. Cover, then transfer to the oven for 2 hours.
  10. Remove from the oven, remove the lid, then return to the oven for another 30 minutes (so 2 1/2 hours in total). Check to ensure lamb is tender - if not, cover and return to the oven.
  11. Remove lamb shanks from the sauce. Skim excess fat off the surface of sauce.
  12. Optional: Strain and discard solids to create a smooth sauce.
  13. Serve the lamb shanks on mashed potato or polenta with plenty of sauce!
